Note: Do not adjust ratio to change cure speed — blend hardener speeds at the correct total ratio (per TDS). Off-ratio mixes cure soft.

Mixing

  • Mix ≥ 2 min; scrape cup walls and base
  • Double-pot for part surfaces: transfer to second cup, remix with fresh stick
  • Record batch masses on the WO

Exotherm

  • Pot life shortens with batch mass; decant promptly
  • Hot or smoking cup: move outside onto concrete; leave until cured
  • Dispose only when cured and cool

Forged CF mix (CS-008 §7, item 4)

Carbon mass ≈ mold cavity volume × 1.4 g/cc. Resin mass ≈ fiber × 1.25. Under-resined charges cure brittle.

Resin Infusion — Process Flow

FEB Composites · CS-006 Rev A · 2026-07
Prerequisites — stack frozen on WO (CS-002 §7.2); mold sealed + released (CS-004); all consumables cut; resin on hand ≈ (dry fiber mass × 0.65) × 1.25 — laminate demand plus mesh/line losses
Dry stack — plies per WO table, marked off as laid; Airtac sparingly (heavy spray inhibits infusion); peel ply over laminate + flange edges
Flow mesh — full part coverage, terminate 30–50 mm before outlet side; inlet-to-outlet distance ≤ 0.5 m, else plan additional feed lines on the WO
Bag — tacky tape perimeter; pleat each corner and every 300–400 mm on curvature; outlet line → resin trap → pump (trap is mandatory); inlet line clamped
Pull vacuum — target ≥ 27 inHg
Drop test (blocker) — isolate pump; loss ≤ 1 inHg over 10 min; record start/end on WO
FAIL → leak-check (tape seams, pleat folds, connector bases, spiral ends) → re-test. Do not mix resin until pass.
Mix — IN2 : AT30 = 100 : 30 by weight (CS-008 table); SLOW hardener unless WO states otherwise
Infuse — inlet line submerged in resin cup; open clamp slowly; monitor flow front for race-tracking; typical 20–30 min small part
Clamp inlet — at resin reaching outlet spiral + full wet-out; pump remains on through gel; record times, masses, gauge readings on WO
Cure / demould — SLOW: 18–24 h @ 25 °C; FAST: 6–8 h. Label per CS-001; weigh; record in WO quality checks; trim per CS-009

Troubleshooting (CS-006 §7.7)

SymptomAction
Gauge won't reach 25 inHgPerimeter seam / connector base / pleat fold — press, re-seat, soapy water on suspects
Holds, then creeps downMicro-leak (spiral end, bag puncture) — patch with tacky-tape square, re-test
Race-tracking along an edgeBriefly clamp inlet; mesh break slows exit; note dam location for next mold
Dry patch, laminate still wetEmergency second feed. If cured: record as defect, assess vs quality checks
Foam/bubbles at inletRe-submerge line; bubbles trap in mesh — note location
Trap fillingClamp inlet sooner next time; empty trap before it reaches the pump port

Wet Layup — Process Flow

FEB Composites · CS-007 Rev A · 2026-07
Prerequisites — stack frozen on WO (CS-002 §7.2); mold sealed + released (CS-004); cloth cut and weighed
Resin quantity — mix ≈ fiber mass × 0.9; squeegee and breather remove the excess (cured part ≈ 60:40 fiber:resin by weight); record mixed vs final mass on WO
Mix — 105 + 206: 5:1 pumps, full strokes; pot life 20–25 min per 100 g @ 72 °F (alternatives: CS-008 table)
Wet out ply-by-ply — per WO ply table; squeegee each ply to uniform sheen, no pooling; mark plies off on WO
Consumables — peel ply over laminate → breather (kept clear of part edges) → VB160 bag, pleats at corners; outlet → resin trap → pump
Vacuum (blocker) — ≥ 25 inHg; drop test: isolate pump, loss ≤ 2 inHg over 10 min; record on WO
FAIL → press seams, re-seat pleats, check connector bases → re-test
Cure — bagged overnight minimum; 206 thin-film solid 10–15 h @ 72 °F. Label (CS-001), weigh, record; trim per CS-009

Foam-wrapped elements

Same flow over shaped foam core. Light foams can crush at full vacuum — reduce as required and record the working level on the WO for that part class.

Note: Gauges read inHg. Full vacuum ≈ 29.9 inHg at sea level (≈ 14.7 psi absolute). References to "pull 30" mean ~30 inHg, i.e. maximum pump vacuum — not 30 psi.

Leak-check sequence

  1. Press all tacky-tape seams
  2. Re-seat pleat folds
  3. Press connector bases through the bag
  4. Inspect spiral-wrap cut ends (tape sharp ends — puncture risk)
  5. Listen; then soapy water on remaining suspects

Line setup

  • Outlet: bag → resin trap → pump. Trap is mandatory in both processes.
  • Inlet (infusion): clamped until drop test passes; line end fully submerged in resin cup.
  • Empty the trap before resin approaches the pump port.

Mold Sealing & Release — XCR System

FEB Composites · CS-004 Rev A · 2026-07

1 — Clean (after machining)

  • Compressed air: all pockets and scribe lines
  • IPA + microfiber until cloth comes away clean; gloves from this point
Caution: No acetone on tooling board. No paper towels or fibrous cloth (embedded fibers mar the surface). Photograph the cleaned mold — reference for scribe-line visibility after sealing.

2 — Seal (XCR)

Mix ratio100 : 41 by weight (2 : 1 by volume)
Batch size≤ 150 g per mix
Pot life8 min @ 20 °C · 4 min @ 30 °C (150 g)

  • 30 lb board typically needs 2 coats; 60 lb often 1
  • Runs/nibs only: local denib ≥ 800 grit, IPA wipe

ShopSabre — Machining Checklist (Tooling Board)

FEB Composites · CS-005 Rev B · 2026-07

Machine

Bed / cut area5×10 ft class (≈ 65.5″ × 122″)
Max cut depth≈ 6″ (Z travel 14″) — stacks taller than 6″ split in CAD per CS-003 §7.1.6; confirm usable depth vs tool length
Hold-downZoned vacuum table through the spoilboard — no tape or nails for sheet stock
ToolingAutomatic tool changer; tool lengths measured automatically (offsets in the controller tool table)

Before booking / before arriving

  1. Reservation on the RFS/RSO site under the operator's own name (own-reservation rule)
  2. Operator certified: RFS training doc + HOT sign-off
  3. CAM generated by the operator; reviewed by Larry
  4. Mold design review initialed on the WO (CS-003 §7.2) — required before stock is glued or a slot booked
  5. Machining time estimate on the WO; slot booked when CAM starts, not when it finishes
  6. Stock glued, fully cured (Ure-Bond 90: 4 h clamp min), labeled: part / WO / up-direction — confirm label matches the job

At the machine

  1. Workholding: open vacuum zones under the part only. Tall stack on a small footprint → firm hand-push test; if it moves, add mechanical fixing through waste margin and keep toolpaths clear of it
  2. Zero Z once per job, from the top of the stock at its highest point — not the bed
  3. Tool changes: ATC handles them — no manual re-zero. Verify CAM tool numbers match the physical rack; re-run the tool-measure cycle for any manually swapped bit
  4. Deep cuts: split toolpaths into depth stages
  5. Verify X/Y zero against stock corner and CAM origin
  6. Dust extraction on; dust mask minimum; max 2 people at the machine during setup
  7. Attend the first 5 minutes; unexpected behavior → stop first, diagnose second

Before leaving

  1. Inspect scribes/surfaces before releasing vacuum (rework is easiest while still located)
  2. Record actual machine time on the WO
  3. Clean bed and room; report tired tooling to Larry
  4. Mold to cleaning + sealing per CS-004; update WO mold location

PPE & Chemical Handling

FEB Composites · SDS: 04 Datasheets · 2026-07
OperationRequired protection
Resin / hardener handling (all systems)Nitrile gloves (change when contaminated); safety glasses when mixing or pouring
Extended or enclosed epoxy work (mold sealing, large layups)Respirator with A-type (organic vapor, EN 14387 A1) cartridges + ventilation. Particulate filters do not stop vapors.
Cutting / sanding cured laminateP100/particulate respirator, dust extraction on, long sleeves, gloves. Second trained person present (team rule).
Machining tooling boardDust mask minimum, extraction on. Clean shared rooms by vacuum, not compressed air.
Aerosols / solvents (Airtac, acetone, clear coat)Ventilation; no ignition sources; return to flammables cabinet
Dry carbon clothGloves — cut edges splinter
Sensitization: all team epoxies are skin and respiratory sensitizers (see SDS). Repeated exposure can cause permanent allergy. Glove discipline is not optional.

Skin contact

  • Wash immediately with soap and water — never solvent
  • Contaminated work clothing stays at the shop (SDS P272)

Exotherm

  • Hot/smoking cup: outside, onto concrete, leave until cured
  • No uncured resin in the trash

Chemical storage

  • Resins/hardeners: chemical boxes / barrel, hardeners on a separate shelf from resins
  • Flammables (Airtac, acetone, clear coat): flammables cabinet
  • No chemicals in food refrigerators

Process Blockers — Hold Points

BlockerWhenRequirementSign-off
1 · Stack freeze

4 · Acceptance criterion

Rule: blocker steps hold up all downstream work-order steps until signed (enforced in the WO app; shaded rows on printed WOs).

Grounding quick reference (CS-010)

AcceptanceEvery probe point ≤ target (default < 5 Ω, or the rules value on the WO) — not a single good point
Probe pattern (default)100 mm grid over conductive zones + every edge midpoint, measured to the grounding point
MeasurementZero/record lead resistance first; grounding hardware fastened as it will fly (torqued, washers)
Mesh in stackCopper mesh continuous to grounding point; 25 mm min overlap between sheets; bare terminable tail at the fastener
Path selectionLarge open laminates: mesh-in-stack. Thin-walled / tapered / wrapped-metal substrates: bonded copper path (epoxy + strip/braid) — do not CF-wrap
On failureBridge with bonded copper strip or add ground tab; re-measure full pattern. Do not sand thin laminate chasing contact.
