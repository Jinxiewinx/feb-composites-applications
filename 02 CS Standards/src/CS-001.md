## 1. Purpose

Unlabeled molds sat blocking shared storage for weeks while nobody was sure whose they were (PP-10), and unlabeled offcuts made "wait, which gsm is this?" a weekly guessing game (PP-09). So: everything physical gets a label that points back to a record.

## 2. Scope

Molds, parts (from demold onward), tooling-board stock, cloth rolls/offcuts, mixed-chemical containers, and storage boxes.

## 3. References

| Ref | Document | Where |
|---|---|---|
| R1 | PP-09, PP-10 | FEB-COMP-PP-001 |
| R2 | CS-013 (the WO the label points to), CS-011 (home locations) | this series |

## 4. Definitions

- **Label** — durable marker (paint pen / sharpie on tape / tag) carrying at minimum: **ID, owner, home location**.

## 5. Equipment & Materials

Paint pens, masking tape, zip tags. Nothing that needs ordering approval.

## 6. Safety

No hazards. Never label directly on a mold's working surface.

## 7. Procedure (outline — expand on first revision)

1. **Molds:** label on a flange/back face at glue-up (CS-003 §7.3): `WO-ID · part name · ME · home location`. Update location line when it moves (matches WO `mold.location`).
2. **Parts:** on demold, tape label inside/on a trim area: `WO-ID · part name`. After trim, move label to a hidden face or keep with the part's bag.
3. **Cloth:** every roll and every usable offcut bag gets gsm + weave + source (e.g. "195 twill Sigmatex"). Unlabeled cloth may not be used on parts (only practice).
4. **Mixed chemicals:** cup/pot labels: contents + mix time + who. Anything unlabeled and curing gets treated as hot waste (CS-008).
5. **Boxes/storage:** box label = contents class + home shelf (CS-011 storage map ID).

## 8. Quality checks / acceptance criteria

| Check | Target | Method | Record where |
|---|---|---|---|
| Every mold in shared space labeled | 100% | Monthly stock walk (CS-011) | Walk notes |
| No unlabeled cloth in the cutting area | 100% | Spot check | — |

## 9. Records

None beyond the labels themselves and WO location fields.
