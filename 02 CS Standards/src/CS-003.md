## 1. Purpose

A mold error you find after machining burns our three scarcest things at once: tooling board, machine time, and calendar. The SN5 clamshell CAD error (raised middle section, Feb 2026) forced a re-glue and re-machine and killed a week of slots in our tightest month (PP-03). This doc collects our mold design rules and adds the one check that would have caught it: someone who didn't design the mold reviews it before any board gets glued or cut.

## 2. Scope

Female/male molds machined from polyurethane tooling board (30–60 lb/ft³) for infusion and wet layup. Appendix A covers 3D-printed compression (forged CF) molds. Hotwire foam molds: out of scope (own project doc until standardized).

**Why Ure-Bond 90:** SN4's board-stacking method (vacuum-bag with laminating resin) was slow and resin-hungry (R2). Ure-Bond 90 (R1) is a purpose-made rigid urethane adhesive: strong bond between boards, ~4 h clamp time, no bagging. Dowels + spot resin remain acceptable for very large stacks where adhesive cost bites.

## 4. Definitions

- **Flange** — flat margin around the part cavity for tacky tape, infusion lines, and trimming reference. 
- **Scribe line** — machined V-groove marking the part trim boundary and datums.
- **Stack (mold stock)** — tooling boards glued into a block thick enough for the mold.
- **Max-Z** — the machine's maximum cut depth; molds taller than max-Z must be split into sections with alignment datums.

## 6. Safety

- **Tooling-board dust** (gluing prep, any hand-shaping): abrasive and irritating — dust mask minimum, respirator + extraction for power sanding (CS-009 PPE table).
- **Ure-Bond 90** (R1 SDS `04 Datasheets/1386A_2-1386B_2.pdf`): two-part urethane — nitrile gloves, ventilated area, no skin contact; cure heat is mild but clamped assemblies stay put for 4 h.
- Heavy stacks: two people to move anything over ~half a sheet; watch pinch points at clamping.

## 7. Procedure

### 7.1 Design rules (CAD)

1. Build the mold body from the part surface per the R3 process (section curves → loft → mirror); keep the machining time target ≤ ~3 h per mold section where practical.
2. **Flanges:** ≥75 mm flat margin beyond the part edge on all sides — tacky tape + spiral tubing + trim allowance need it (SN4 lesson: insufficient side stock, R2).
3. **Scribe lines:** machine part-boundary scribes parallel to ribs/features plus two datum scribes; they must survive sealing (CS-004 §7.3.5).
4. **No overhangs** — 3-axis machining only; check every face has positive draft from the machining direction. Target ≥2° draft on deep walls.
5. **Foam support:** no unsupported sections thinner than 15 mm — tooling board snaps (R3). Fillet internal corners ≥ the finishing-tool radius.
6. **Max-Z check:** if mold height exceeds machine max-Z, split into sections in CAD with dowel/datum features designed in, not improvised at the machine (R2).
7. **Attachment/mounting points:** where the part needs marked hole locations (seat lesson, Oct 2025 meeting notes), design small conical divots into the mold surface.

### 7.2 Design review — the blocker (PP-03)

1. Before any stock is glued or any slot booked, a reviewer who is NOT the mold's designer walks this checklist against the CAD (10 minutes):

| # | Check |
|---|---|
| 1 | Part surface matches current part CAD revision (open both; overlay) |
| 2 | Flanges ≥75 mm all around |
| 3 | Scribe + datum lines present |
| 4 | No overhangs / negative draft from machining direction |
| 5 | No sections <15 mm; internal fillets ≥ tool radius |
| 6 | Height ≤ machine max-Z (value in CS-005 §5), or splits + alignment features designed |
| 7 | Stock size exists (check inventory) and stack plan drawn |
| 8 | Machining time estimate written on the WO |
| 9 | Stack frozen per CS-002 §7.2 (mold offsets depend on it) |

2. Reviewer initials the WO "Mold design review" step. **No initials, no glue, no booking.**

### 7.3 Stock gluing

1. Dry-fit boards; mark orientation. Faces must be dust-free (compressed air + IPA wipe).
2. Ure-Bond 90 per R1: mix per TB, spread thin/even on one face, mate, clamp evenly, **4 h clamp minimum**; full cure per TB before machining. For stacks >3 boards or very large molds: dowels + spot resin alternative (R2).
3. Label the stack immediately (part, WO, up-direction — CS-001).

### 7.4 Machine, clean, seal

1. Machine per CS-005 (zeroing rules live there).
2. Clean and seal per CS-004. Update the WO mold record (layers, density, sealing, location).

## Appendix A — 3D-printed compression (forged CF) molds

From the SN5 *Forged/Compression Molded Carbon Fiber* study: PLA/PETG molds (40–50% infill, 4–5 walls, 0.1 mm layers) are free at Jacobs but **deform/crack at ~50–100 psi and soften at 60–100 °C** — no hydraulic press, no heated cure. Design: piston-into-cavity, stroke ≈ 2× part depth, 0.1 mm sliding-face offset, chamfered split points for wedging, vent grooves against hydrolock. Clamp math: four 600 lbf trigger clamps ≈ 2,400 lbf spread over the part area → ~150 psi at 4×4 in (16 in²) but only ~37 psi at 8×8 in (64 in²). Read the two limits together: parts must be small (≲16–25 in²) for clamps to even *reach* the 50–100 psi consolidation target — and at exactly those pressures a printed mold is at its cracking threshold. So for small forged parts, always distribute the load with through-bolts + aluminum backup plates rather than bare trigger clamps (whose pads also relax over time); for anything larger, clamps cannot reach target pressure at all — redesign, or accept low-pressure quality. First-part failure mode to avoid: resin starvation (see CS-008 mix math).
